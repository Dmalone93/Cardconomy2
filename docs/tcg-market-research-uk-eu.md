## 1. Market Size

### Europe
- **European TCG market: ~$1.34B (2025), projected $1.45B (2026)**, growing at 7.6% CAGR to $2.82B by 2035
- Physical TCG segment specifically: ~$1.12B in 2025, growing at 7.2% CAGR
- Germany and the UK lead in competitive play and collector activity
- France is an emerging collector hub (Paris Card Show drew 2,200+ paying attendees)

**Source:** [GM Insights — Europe Trading Card Games Market](https://www.gminsights.com/industry-analysis/europe-trading-card-games-market)

### UK Specifically
- UK "Card Games" market revenue: ~$130M (2025) with 4.89% annual growth projected to 2029
- This figure covers all card games, not TCG-specific — the TCG-specific UK figure is not cleanly separated in public data
- UK and Germany are the two largest European TCG markets

**Source:** [Statista — Card Games UK](https://www.statista.com/outlook/cmo/toys-hobby/toys-games/card-games/united-kingdom)

### Global (for context)
- Estimates vary widely: $6.6B to $15.1B depending on source and what they include (digital vs physical, collectible vs playable)
- Most credible range for physical TCG only: **$8-13B globally (2025)**

**Sources:** [Mordor Intelligence](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/trading-card-game-market), [GM Insights](https://www.gminsights.com/industry-analysis/trading-card-games-market), [Cognitive Market Research](https://www.cognitivemarketresearch.com/trading-card-games-market-report)

### Key takeaway
The European TCG market is roughly **$1.3-1.5 billion**, not "multi-billion." The UK is a significant slice but there is no clean public figure for TCG-only UK revenue. Safe to say the UK TCG market is in the **hundreds of millions of dollars** range.

## 2. Competitor Platforms — Verified Fees & Data

### eBay UK (2026)

**Fee structure (business sellers):**
- Final Value Fee: **9.9%–14.9%** (category-dependent)
- Per-order fee: **£0.30 or £0.40**
- Regulatory Operating Fee: **0.35%**
- 20% VAT on fees (reclaimable if VAT-registered)
- **All-in for a typical trading card sale: ~12-15%**

**Private sellers:** eBay now charges the buyer a Buyer Protection Fee instead of charging sellers FVF directly — but this effectively raises the price buyers see.

**High-value cards:** 50% discount on FVF for cards sold at $1,000+ (up to $7,500 portion).

**Key change (Feb 2026):** Per-order FVF increased for orders above £10.

**Source:** [eBay UK Seller Centre — Rate Card Change Jan 2026](https://www.ebay.co.uk/sellercentre/news/2026-january/rate-card-change), [Frooition eBay Fee Calculator UK 2026](https://www.frooition.com/ebay-fee-calculator-uk/)

### Cardmarket (2026)

**Fee structure:**
- Private Sellers: **5% commission**
- Professional Sellers: **3% commission**
- Powersellers: **1.5% commission**
- Commission is on item price only (not shipping), capped at **€100 per article**
- Trustee Service: **0.5%–1%** of article value
- Currency conversion: **3%**
- Instant credit: **€0.35 + 5%**
- **All-in for a typical EU-domestic sale: ~6-8%**

**Revenue:** Cardmarket generated **~$566M revenue on cardmarket.com in 2025**, with 10-15% growth projected for 2026. This was a 20-25% increase from the prior year.

**Brexit impact on UK sellers:**
- UK buyers from EU sellers face **20% import VAT** plus handling fees on orders above £135
- UK sellers shipping to EU face reverse paperwork issues
- UK collectors increasingly transact domestically on Cardmarket
- Post-Brexit friction effectively killed economic viability for most low-value cross-border single-card trades

**Source:** [CardPulse — Cardmarket Complete Guide 2026](https://www.cardpulse.club/blog/cardmarket-complete-guide-2026), [Flipzi — Cardmarket Fee Calculator 2026](https://flipzi.io/cardmarket-fee-calculator/), [ecdb — Cardmarket Revenue](https://ecdb.com/resources/sample-data/retailer/cardmarket)

### Whatnot (2026)

**Fee structure (UK):**
- Commission: **6.67% + VAT** (most categories)
- Coins & Money: **4% + VAT**
- Payment processing: **2.42% + VAT + £0.25 + VAT per transaction**
- **All-in for a typical UK sale: ~11-12% including VAT**
- No listing fees
- **High-value promotion:** 0% commission on the portion above **£1,500** per order (since Jan 2026)

**Growth data:**
- **$8 billion GMV** in live sales during 2025 (doubled from prior year)
- UK sellers increased **360% year-on-year**
- European seller base grew **600% year-on-year**
- France adding new sellers fastest: **888% growth**
- UK: viewers watched **5 million+ hours** of livestream shopping in 2025
- Europe-wide: **340,000 hours of livestreams watched weekly**
- UK daily sellers averaging **£30K/month**
- At least **10 trading cards sell every minute** on the platform

**Source:** [Whatnot — Europe Live Selling Report](https://blog.teamwhatnot.com/united-kingdom-blog/livesellingreport), [Whatnot Fees 2026](https://www.voolist.com/blog/whatnot-fees-2026), [Whatnot Help — Seller Fees](https://help.whatnot.com/hc/en-us/articles/4847069165965-Whatnot-Seller-Fees-and-Commissions-Schedule)

**Key change (Feb 2026):** Commission increased from 10.25% to 10.75%.

## 5. Key Market Problems (Verified)

### Counterfeiting
- **125% increase** in counterfeit cards reported in 2025 fraud reports
- Counterfeits now appear in raw singles, sealed products, **and graded slabs**
- Facebook Marketplace is a primary distribution channel for "super-fakes" in the UK
- UV light testing, weight testing, and grading service verification are the main detection methods
- Global authentication services market projected to reach **$6.6B by 2033**

**Source:** [Card Chill — Counterfeit Detection 2026](https://cardchill.com/article/pokemon-cards-counterfeit-detection-advanced-verification-techniques-for-2026-collectors), [GlobeNewsWire — Authentication Services Market](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2025/06/05/3094641/0/en/Global-Trading-Card-Game-Authentication-Services-Market-Size-to-Surpass-US-6-611-93-Million-By-2033-Astute-Analytica.html)

### Post-Brexit Cross-Border Friction
- UK-EU shipping went from frictionless to frustrating post-2021 and **remains so in 2026**
- UK buyers from EU Cardmarket sellers: 20% import VAT + handling fees (orders above £135)
- UK sellers to EU: customs paperwork and reverse charges
- Result: UK TCG collectors increasingly transact domestically
- This creates a gap: no dominant UK-domestic TCG marketplace exists

**Source:** [CardPulse — Cardmarket Guide 2026](https://www.cardpulse.club/blog/cardmarket-complete-guide-2026)

### Bulk Selling Friction
- Bulk commons from modern sets have little to no secondary market value
- No platform makes it easy to sell a mixed collection of 500+ cards
- Ungraded cards represent **33% of secondary market circulation**, creating trust and valuation challenges
- Print oversupply affected **27% of collector confidence**

**Source:** [PACKRAT — How to Sell Trading Cards UK](https://packratt.co.uk/guides/how-to-sell-trading-cards-uk)

### Retail Closures
- GAME (UK chain) closed all standalone stores by April 2026 after entering administration
- Once operated ~300 stores, now only exists as concessions inside Sports Direct/House of Fraser
- Independent LGS numbers are not publicly tracked but Geek Retreat (franchise chain) operates ~18 locations
- Independent shops face channel disintermediation: collectors bypassing retail for direct-to-consumer and online

**Source:** [Retail Gazette — GAME Closures](https://www.retailgazette.co.uk/blog/2026/03/game-closes-final-standalone-stores-as-high-street-presence-ends/), [Nintendo Life — GAME Administration](https://www.nintendolife.com/news/2026/02/uk-retailer-game-closes-all-standalone-stores-as-it-enters-administration)

## 6. Opportunity Summary

**The gap Cardconomy fills:**

1. **No dominant UK-domestic TCG marketplace.** Cardmarket is EU-centric with Brexit friction. eBay is generic. TCGplayer is US-only. Card Synced and PACKRAT are emerging but early-stage.

2. **No platform connects online and in-person.** All existing platforms are shipping-only. In-person trading at LGS, meetups, and shows has zero digital infrastructure.

3. **Listing friction is the #1 seller barrier.** PACKRAT has AI scanning (a direct competitor feature). No other UK platform offers it. This is table stakes, not a unique differentiator — execution and depth of the tool matters.

4. **LGS have no digital tools.** GAME is gone. Independent shops have no submission queue, no digital buylist, no counter interface. Cardconomy fills this with purpose-built shop tools.

5. **Post-Brexit created a domestic vacuum.** UK collectors are increasingly UK-only in their buying. There is no UK-native marketplace with the depth of Cardmarket's catalog and pricing transparency.
