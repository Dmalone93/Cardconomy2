# Cardconomy — Trade Show Deck

> **Purpose:** Familiarisation guide + demo talking points for the in-person trade show. Covers what we built, why it matters, and how it differs from eBay, Cardmarket, Whatnot, and TCGplayer.

---

## The One-Liner

**Cardconomy is the marketplace that connects collectors, sellers, and local game stores in one platform — with tools none of the incumbents offer.**

---

## The Problem

The trading card market is growing rapidly but the buying and selling experience is fragmented and frustrating:

| Pain Point | Who Feels It | Current Reality |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Listing friction | Sellers | Photographing, describing, and pricing 500 cards takes days on eBay |
| No local trade infrastructure | Everyone | In-person trades happen in car parks with no safety, verification, or dispute resolution |
| Game stores are invisible | Stores | LGS can't compete with online marketplaces for walk-in sellers or buylist traffic |
| Price opacity | Buyers | You need 3 tabs open to know if a price is fair |
| Collection blindness | Collectors | No single place to track what you own, what it's worth, and when to sell |
| Trust gap | Everyone | Fakes are rampant, seller verification is weak, buyer protection varies |

## The Three Big Differentiators

### 1. Local-First Infrastructure
No other marketplace connects online and in-person. Cardconomy has:
- Verified shop network with storefronts
- Safe trade meetup system at local shops
- Digital submission queue (scan at home, review at counter)
- Shops as authentication centers

**Why it matters:** A significant share of high-value card trades still happen in person. Everyone else ignores this.

### 2. Scan-to-Sell in Seconds
LiveSweep + single card scan removes the #1 barrier to selling:
- Point, identify, auto-price, publish
- Batch pricing strategies for bulk
- Triage system for shops (flagged/buylist/singles/bulk)

**Why it matters:** The average eBay listing takes 8+ minutes per card. LiveSweep does cards in seconds each.

### 3. Three Dashboards, Three Personas
Every user type gets a purpose-built command center:
- **Buyers:** Portfolio tracker + price alerts + buylist matching
- **Sellers:** Earnings + active listing management + offer alerts
- **Stores:** Revenue + submission queue + buylist performance

**Why it matters:** eBay, Cardmarket, and TCGplayer give everyone the same seller dashboard. Cardconomy recognises that a collector checking their portfolio, a seller shipping orders, and a shop grading walk-ins have completely different jobs to do.

## FAQ / Objections

**"This is just another marketplace"**
> No — it's the only one with local shop infrastructure. Every other marketplace is shipping-only. We connect the trades that happen in person.

**"Why would a shop use this?"**
> Free deal flow. Sellers scan their cards before they walk in. The shop reviews from the counter, makes offers, and never has to manually sort a stack again. It's a free upgrade to their intake process.

**"How do you compete with eBay's volume?"**
> We don't compete head-on. We win on specialisation — card-specific tools (scanning, pricing, grading, authentication) that eBay will never build, plus the local network they can't replicate.

**"What about Whatnot?"**
> Different use case. Whatnot is entertainment and impulse buying. We're for considered purchases — when someone wants to know the market price, compare sellers, and make an informed decision. Both can coexist.

**"Is this just a prototype?"**
> This is a fully interactive prototype with working navigation, state persistence, and realistic data. Every flow can be walked through end to end. We're demonstrating the product vision, not just mockups.
