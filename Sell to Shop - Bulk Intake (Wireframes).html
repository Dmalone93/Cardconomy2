A · Live Sweep Scan

Hold the phone over the pile and flip through. The camera stays open and recognizes each card the instant its face is visible — no tap per card. A running counter and a thumbnail tray confirm captures in real time.
Default for big, unsorted lots
  • No per-card interaction — pure throughput
  • Blanket condition (e.g. "NM") applied automatically
  • AI flags creases / whitening for review only
  • Haptic buzz + sound on each successful capture
  • Struggles with heavy glare / sleeved cards — prompts to deslevee
⏱ ~25 min / 1,000 🎯 best for semi-sorted stacks

B · Batch Fan Photo

Lay 9–12 cards in a fan or grid, snap a single photo, and the model splits and identifies all of them at once. Repeat per handful. Fewer captures than per-card, more layout effort than sweep.
Binder pages & tidy rows
  • Photograph a full 9-pocket binder page in one shot
  • Good lighting control vs. a moving sweep
  • Re-snap a single mis-read without redoing the batch
  • Requires table space to lay cards out
